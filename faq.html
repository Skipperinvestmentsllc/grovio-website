Getting Started

What is Grovio, exactly?

Grovio is a homeschool companion app that helps you track attendance, plan your family's daily rhythm, build a learning portfolio, and access practical articles through The Grovio Guide. It's designed for homeschool parents who want a simple, warm tool — not another complicated system to manage.

Is Grovio free?

Grovio is free to get started. As the app grows, some advanced features may move to a paid plan, but there will always be a meaningful free experience and you'll never be surprised by a charge.

Do I need to create an account?

You don't. You can use Grovio without an account, and your data will stay on your device only. However, creating an account lets you save your data to the cloud so it's backed up and accessible if you switch devices. We'd recommend signing in if you plan to use Grovio regularly.

How do I create an account?

Tap "Sign In" and enter your email address. We'll send a 6-digit code to your inbox — enter it in the app and you're in. No password needed, ever. If you don't see the email, check your spam or promotions folder. Sign in with Apple is coming soon for an even faster one-tap sign-in experience.

I never got my sign-in code. What do I do?

First, double-check your spam, junk, and promotions folders — that's where it lands most often. Make sure you entered your email address correctly (it's easy to miss a typo). If you're using a school or workplace email, those servers sometimes block automated emails. Try again with a personal email like Gmail or iCloud. If it's still not arriving, email us at hello@grovioapp.com and we'll help you troubleshoot.

Can I use Grovio on my phone and my computer?

Yes. Grovio is available as an iOS app and as a web app you can use in any browser. When you're signed in, your data syncs across both, so you can add a portfolio photo from your phone and review your planner on your laptop later.

Setting Up Your Family

How do I add my kids to Grovio?

During onboarding, Grovio will walk you through adding your learners. You'll enter each child's name and some basic details like age or grade level. You can always add, edit, or remove learners later from within the app. All learner information is entered by you — your kids never need their own accounts.

I homeschool multiple children. Can I manage them all in one account?

Absolutely. Grovio is built with multi-learner families in mind. You add each child as a separate learner under your single parent account, and their attendance, rhythm, and portfolio data are all tracked individually. You don't need a separate account for each child.

Can two parents share the same account?

Right now, Grovio accounts are tied to a single email address. The simplest approach is for both parents to use the same login — you'll both request the 6-digit sign-in code to the same email when needed. Shared or family accounts with separate logins are something we're considering for the future, but it's not available yet.

Attendance

How does attendance tracking work?

Grovio's attendance tracker lets you mark each day as a school day with a single tap. You can also log field trip days, which count toward your attendance total. At a glance, you can see how many days you've completed — helpful for staying on track with your state's requirements.

How do I export my attendance records?

Go to the Record tab, tap the export button in the top right corner, and choose your format. Grovio generates a clean PDF attendance record you can save, print, or share — formatted for compliance purposes so it's ready if you ever need to present it to an evaluator or administrator.

Do sick days count toward my attendance total?

No. Sick days are tracked separately and do not count toward your attendance total. This is intentional — you should be able to log an honest record of your year without worrying that a stomach bug is throwing off your numbers.

Do field trips count as school days?

Yes. When you log a field trip day, it counts toward your attendance total. Field trips are real learning, and Grovio treats them that way.

Can I edit or undo an attendance entry?

Yes. If you accidentally marked the wrong day or need to make a correction, you can tap on any attendance entry to update or remove it. Mistakes happen — that's totally normal.

How many school days does my state require?

This varies by state and sometimes by district. Grovio does not provide legal advice, but The Grovio Guide includes articles on compliance and record-keeping that can point you in the right direction. For your specific situation, your state's Department of Education website or a local homeschool association are the best resources.

Daily Rhythm & Planner

What is a "daily rhythm" and how is it different from a schedule?

A schedule is rigid — math at 9:00, reading at 10:00. A rhythm is the natural flow of your day: morning time, then focused work, then outside time, then lunch. Grovio's planner is built around rhythms because that's how most homeschool days actually work. You set the shape of your day, not a minute-by-minute agenda.

How do I set up my planner?

During onboarding, you'll be guided through setting up your family's rhythm — adding anchor activities (the big beats of your day), subjects, and any routines that structure your time. You can adjust everything after setup, so don't feel pressure to get it perfect on the first try. Your rhythm will evolve, and Grovio is built to evolve with it.

Can I plan different schedules for different kids?

Each learner has their own rhythm and subject data, so you can customize the daily flow for each child individually. If your kids share some activities (like morning read-aloud) but diverge for others (like math levels), Grovio can handle that.

Can I see a weekly or monthly view of my plan?

Grovio's planner is currently focused on the daily view, which aligns with how most homeschool families actually plan — one day at a time. Expanded views are something we're exploring for the future.

Portfolio

What is the portfolio for?

The portfolio is a place to capture and save evidence of your child's learning — photos of projects, notes about breakthroughs, descriptions of activities, field trip memories. Think of it as a keepsake, not an audit. Some families also use it to satisfy state evaluation or documentation requirements, but at its heart, it's a record of the beautiful, messy, real work your kids are doing.

What can I add to a portfolio entry?

Each entry can include a photo, a written note or description, and relevant details like the date and subject area. You capture entries using the camera or photo library on your device. Keep it simple — a quick snapshot of a drawing, a sentence about what clicked during a math lesson, a photo from a nature walk. It doesn't need to be polished.

Where are my photos stored?

When you're signed in, portfolio photos are stored securely in Supabase Storage (a cloud platform based in the United States). Photos are compressed on your device before uploading to save storage space and bandwidth. If you're using Grovio without an account, photos stay on your device only.

Can I share portfolio entries with someone else?

Yes. Grovio lets you create shareable links to portfolio entries so you can show them to a family member, an evaluator, or anyone you choose. Sharing is always opt-in — nothing is shared unless you specifically create a link. You control who sees what, and you can deactivate any sharing link at any time directly within the app.

Can I delete a portfolio entry?

Yes. You can delete any portfolio entry at any time. Once deleted, the entry and its associated photo are permanently removed.

The Grovio Guide

What is The Grovio Guide?

The Grovio Guide is a library of practical articles written for homeschool parents — especially those who are newer to homeschooling or looking for reassurance that they're on the right track. Articles cover topics like getting started, building a daily routine, handling resistance, and understanding state requirements. They're written by Claire, Grovio's guide voice, in a warm and grounded tone.

Is the Guide included for free?

Yes. The Grovio Guide is available to all users at no cost. You can also read all Guide articles on the web at grovioapp.com/guide without downloading the app.

Does The Grovio Guide give legal advice about my state?

No. The Guide offers general information and practical tips about compliance and record-keeping, but it does not replace legal advice specific to your state or situation. Articles covering compliance topics include a disclaimer to that effect. For legal questions, please consult your state's Department of Education or a qualified professional.

Account, Privacy & Data

What data does Grovio collect?

When you create an account, Grovio collects your email address for sign-in purposes. Beyond that, Grovio stores the data you choose to enter: learner names and details, attendance records, planner data, and portfolio entries (including photos). Grovio does not sell your data, does not run ads, and does not share your information with advertising partners. For full details, see our Privacy Policy.

Is my data safe?

Grovio takes your privacy seriously. All data is encrypted in transit (HTTPS) and at rest in our database. We use one-time code authentication so no passwords are ever stored. Your data is hosted in the United States through Supabase and Vercel, both of which maintain robust security practices.

Can I delete my account and all my data?

Yes. To delete your account and all associated data, email hello@grovioapp.com with your request. We will process the deletion within 30 days, after which your account, learner information, attendance records, planner data, and portfolio entries (including photos) will be permanently removed from our servers.

Do my kids need their own accounts?

No. Grovio is designed for parents. You manage everything from your single account. Your children never need to sign in, create an account, or interact with the app directly. All learner data is entered and controlled by you.

Troubleshooting

The app looks different on my phone than on the web. Is that normal?

Grovio is designed to work across devices, but the layout may adapt to your screen size. If something looks off or broken, try refreshing the page (on web) or closing and reopening the app (on iOS). If the issue persists, email us at hello@grovioapp.com with a screenshot and we'll look into it.

My data isn't syncing between my phone and computer.

Make sure you're signed in on both devices with the same email address. Data only syncs to the cloud when you have an account — if you've been using Grovio without signing in, your data is stored locally on that device only. If you're signed in and still experiencing issues, try signing out and back in. If the problem continues, reach out to us at hello@grovioapp.com.

The app is running slowly or freezing.

Try closing and reopening the app, or refreshing the browser. If you're on iOS, make sure you're running the latest version of the app. Large portfolios with many photos can occasionally cause slowness on older devices. If performance issues continue, let us know at hello@grovioapp.com and include your device type so we can investigate.

I accidentally deleted something. Can I get it back?

Unfortunately, once an entry is deleted, it's permanently removed. We're exploring the idea of a brief undo window for deletions in a future update, but for now, deletions are final.
