Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2026

What Grovio collects

Grovio collects information you provide directly — including your email address when you create an account, and the homeschool records, portfolio entries, and attendance data you choose to save. We also collect basic usage data to improve the app.

How we use your information

Your data is used to provide and improve Grovio. We use your email address to send you a sign-in link when you request one. We do not sell your personal information to third parties. We do not use your data for advertising.

Your children's information

Grovio is designed for parents to manage their own homeschool records. Any information about children is entered by the parent and stored securely. We do not knowingly collect personal information directly from children. Parents are responsible for the information they choose to enter.

Data storage

When you use Grovio without an account, your data is stored locally on your device only. When you create an account, your data is stored securely using Supabase, a third-party database provider. Photos are stored using Supabase Storage. All data is encrypted in transit and at rest.

Data deletion

You can delete your account and all associated data at any time by contacting us at hello@grovioapp.com. We will process deletion requests within 30 days.

Third-party services

Grovio uses the following third-party services: Supabase (database and authentication), Vercel (hosting), and Apple TestFlight/App Store (app distribution). Each has their own privacy policy governing their data practices.

Sharing links

When you generate a portfolio sharing link, anyone with that link can view the portfolio content you have chosen to share. You can delete sharing links at any time from within the app.

Changes to this policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. We will notify users of significant changes via the app or email.

Contact

Questions about this policy? Email us at hello@grovioapp.com.
