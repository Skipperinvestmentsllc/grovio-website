Introduction — Who We Are

Grovio is a homeschool companion app designed to help families track attendance, plan their daily rhythm, build a learning portfolio, and access educational articles through The Grovio Guide. Grovio is built and operated by Sondra Kilian, doing business as Skipperinvestmentsllc, based in the United States.

This privacy policy explains what information Grovio collects, how that information is used, and what choices you have. It is written in plain English on purpose — you should not need a law degree to understand how your data is handled. If anything in this policy is unclear, please reach out at hello@grovioapp.com and I will be happy to explain.

This policy applies to both the Grovio web application (hosted at grovioapp.com) and the Grovio iOS application. By creating an account or using the app, you agree to the practices described below.

Information We Collect

Grovio collects only the information needed to provide you with a useful homeschool companion. There are two ways you can use Grovio, and the data collected depends on which you choose.

Without an account: You can use Grovio without creating an account. In this case, all data you enter — including any learner information, planner entries, or attendance records — stays on your device only. Grovio does not transmit or store this data on any server.

With an account: When you create an account, Grovio collects your email address for authentication purposes. Once signed in, you may also enter the following information, all of which is stored on our servers: learner names and basic details (such as age or grade level) as entered by you; attendance records including dates marked as school days, sick days, and field trips; portfolio entries including photos, notes, and descriptions of your learner's work; planner and rhythm data including subjects, schedules, and daily routines; and any preferences or settings you configure within the app.

Grovio also collects basic app usage data through its hosting and database infrastructure (Vercel and Supabase). This includes standard server logs such as IP addresses, browser or device type, and timestamps of requests. This data is used solely for maintaining and improving the app's performance and is not used for advertising, profiling, or behavioral tracking of any kind.

How We Use Your Information

Grovio uses the information you provide for a short list of straightforward purposes: to authenticate your account and keep it secure via magic link email; to store and display your attendance records, planner data, portfolio entries, and learner information so you can access them across sessions and devices; to generate and display portfolio sharing links when you choose to create them; and to maintain, troubleshoot, and improve the Grovio app.

Grovio does not use your information for advertising. Grovio does not sell your personal data to any third party. Grovio does not build behavioral profiles or use your data for any purpose beyond operating the app as described in this policy.

Children's Privacy

Grovio is designed for use by parents and guardians — not by children. The app is directed at adults who are managing their family's homeschool experience. While the app stores information about learners (such as names and portfolio entries), all of this information is entered and managed by a parent or guardian. Grovio does not knowingly collect personal information directly from children under the age of 13.

Because the app is parent-directed, Grovio does not require verifiable parental consent under the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) for the information parents choose to enter about their children. Parents maintain full control over what learner information they add and can request deletion of any or all of this data at any time.

Data Storage and Security

When you create an account, your data is stored in Supabase, a cloud database platform with servers located in the United States. Photos uploaded to your portfolio are stored in Supabase Storage. The Grovio web application is hosted on Vercel, also based in the United States.

All data transmitted between your device and Grovio's servers is encrypted in transit using HTTPS/TLS. Data stored in Supabase is encrypted at rest. Grovio uses one-time code authentication — when you sign in, a 6-digit code is sent to your email address. You enter it directly in the app. No password is ever created or stored.

While Grovio takes reasonable measures to protect your information, no system connected to the internet can guarantee absolute security. If you ever believe your account has been compromised, please contact hello@grovioapp.com immediately.

Data Sharing

Grovio does not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. Grovio does not share your data with advertising partners because Grovio has no advertising partners. There are no ads in the app and there never will be.

Grovio does rely on a small number of third-party service providers to operate the app. These providers have access to your data only to the extent necessary to provide their services: Supabase (supabase.com/privacy) provides authentication, database, and file storage services; Vercel (vercel.com/legal/privacy-policy) provides web application hosting; and Apple (apple.com/legal/privacy) provides iOS app distribution through the App Store. Each of these providers maintains their own privacy policies and security practices.

Grovio may also disclose your information if required to do so by law, such as in response to a valid subpoena, court order, or government request.

Portfolio Sharing Links

Grovio allows you to create shareable links to portfolio entries so you can show your learner's work to family members, evaluators, or others you choose. This feature is entirely opt-in — no portfolio content is publicly accessible unless you specifically generate a sharing link.

When you create a sharing link, anyone who has that link can view the shared portfolio content. You are responsible for deciding who you share these links with. You can deactivate any sharing link at any time directly within the app — once deactivated, the link will no longer display portfolio content to anyone who opens it. You can also generate a new link at any time after deactivating an existing one.

Your Rights and Data Deletion

You have the right to access, correct, or delete the personal information Grovio holds about you. Within the app, you can view and edit your learner information, attendance records, planner data, and portfolio entries at any time.

If you would like to delete your account and all associated data, you can do so by emailing hello@grovioapp.com. Grovio will process your deletion request within 30 days. Once deletion is complete, your account information, learner data, attendance records, planner data, and portfolio entries (including photos) will be permanently removed from Grovio's servers.

If you are located in a jurisdiction that grants additional privacy rights — such as the right to data portability, the right to restrict processing, or rights under the European General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) or the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) — please contact hello@grovioapp.com and Grovio will make a good-faith effort to honor your request.

Changes to This Policy

Grovio may update this privacy policy from time to time to reflect changes in the app, legal requirements, or business practices. When changes are made, the "Last updated" date at the top of this policy will be revised. If a change is material — for example, if Grovio begins collecting a new type of information or sharing data with a new service provider — Grovio will make reasonable efforts to notify you, such as through an in-app notice or an email to your registered address.

Your continued use of Grovio after any changes take effect constitutes your acceptance of the updated policy. If you do not agree with a change, you may stop using the app and request deletion of your data.
