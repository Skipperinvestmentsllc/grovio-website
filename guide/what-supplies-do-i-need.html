🌱 Getting Started

What supplies do I actually need to begin?

C

Claire

3 min read · The Grovio Guide

I'm going to save you from something I did our first year: buying everything.

I walked into a teacher supply store and left with bins, organizers, charts — all of it.

Most of it never got used.

Here's what you actually need at the beginning: something to write with, something to write on, and a library card.

That's it.

For the first few weeks, that will take you further than you expect.

If your child is younger, a simple workbook for math or handwriting can be helpful — not because it's required, but because some kids like having something concrete to complete. For older kids, you may not need much beyond books and a notebook.

What ended up mattering more than supplies was how I kept track of what we were doing. Honestly, I handle that through Grovio now — it's where our rhythm, attendance, and portfolio all live. It keeps things from piling up in my head. If you prefer paper, a simple folder works just as well.

What I'd avoid buying right away: anything labeled "complete," expensive kits, or systems you don't fully understand yet.

Start simple. You'll know what you need soon enough.
