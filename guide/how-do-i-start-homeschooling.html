🌱 Getting Started

How do I start homeschooling my child?

Claire

3 min read · The Grovio Guide

Okay, first — if you're asking this question, you're already doing better than you think.

I remember standing in my kitchen after we made the decision, thinking, "Now what?" I thought I needed a plan. A curriculum. A schedule that looked official.

I had none of it.

And honestly? That ended up being okay.

Here's the thing — starting homeschooling isn't about getting everything right from day one. It's about taking the first step without needing the whole picture figured out.

The only thing I would do right away is check your state's legal requirements. Not because it's complicated — most of the time it's surprisingly simple — but because it gives your brain somewhere solid to land. It quiets that "am I doing this wrong?" feeling.

After that, give yourself a little breathing room.

Those first weeks don't need to look like school. Ours didn't. We read together, spent time outside, and talked more than we ever had. It felt slow, but something important was happening underneath it.

If you're not sure what to do next, don't build a full plan.

Just start with one thing.

Read a book together. Follow a question your child asks. See what holds their attention.

That's enough for now. The rest builds from there — much more naturally than you expect.
