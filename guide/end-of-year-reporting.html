📋 Compliance & Record Keeping

What do I need for end-of-year reporting?

C

Claire

4 min read · The Grovio Guide

I'm not a lawyer — please verify with your state's official requirements.

End-of-year reporting has a way of looming larger in your mind than it does in practice.

My first year, I left it too late. I spent a week trying to reconstruct what we'd done from memory and scattered notes.

After that, I changed my approach entirely.

Now the end of year is almost anticlimactic — because everything is already in one place before I even sit down to compile it.

Some states require nothing at year's end. Others want attendance records, a subject summary, or evidence of learning. A smaller number require a formal portfolio review or standardized test.

For our family, Grovio handles most of this automatically — attendance is logged daily, and when reporting comes around I can export a clean attendance record directly from the app. If a portfolio review is required, I used to dread the printing and organizing. Now I just send a link — the evaluator sees everything, organized and timestamped, without me touching a printer.

For families keeping paper records, a printed attendance log, a simple monthly subject note, and a folder of work samples collected through the year will cover the same ground.

The most important thing is to look up your state's requirements now — not in May. It takes about ten minutes and completely changes how you feel about the whole process.
